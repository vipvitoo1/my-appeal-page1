What is trademark infringement?
Generally, trademark infringement occurs when all three of the following requirements are met:
  • A company or person uses a trademark owned by someone else (or a similar mark) without permission.
  • This use is in the course of trade, meaning it is in connection with the sale or promotion of goods or services.
  • This use is likely to cause consumer confusion about the source, endorsement, or affiliation of the goods or services.

The core standard for trademark infringement is often the likelihood of confusion. There are many factors that determine whether someone's use of a trademark may cause confusion. For example, when someone uses a trademark on unrelated goods or services, it may not be infringement because it may not cause confusion. It is also often important to consider which party used the trademark first.
